AI Project Management: How Small Teams Can Work Like Enterprise

You don't need a big budget or a dedicated IT team to run projects like a pro. These AI-powered tools bring enterprise-level project management to small teams — without the enterprise price tag.

Project management software used to be a trade-off. Free tools were too simple. Enterprise tools (like Jira or Asana) were powerful but expensive and complicated. Small teams got stuck with either too little or too much.

AI has changed that. Modern project management tools use AI to automate the boring parts — assigning tasks, summarising progress, flagging risks — so you don't need a dedicated project manager. Here are the best ones for small teams.

The Top Contenders

ClickUp

  • AI auto-assigns tasks
  • Summarises project status
  • Free tier for up to 5 users
  • Replaces 5 tools in one

Notion

  • AI writing built into docs
  • Best for knowledge management
  • Great for documentation

ClickUp — Best All-Round Project Management

ClickUp is our top pick for small teams. It combines tasks, docs, goals, and chat into one platform. The AI features aren't gimmicks — they genuinely help: automatically assigning tasks based on workload, summarising long project threads, and suggesting workflow improvements.

The free tier handles most small teams comfortably. Paid plans start at €9/month, which replaces tools costing 5x as much.

Notion — Best for Documentation & Knowledge

Notion is less of a pure project management tool and more of a "company brain" that happens to include project management. The AI add-on makes it genuinely powerful — it can summarise meeting notes, generate action items, write draft documents, and answer questions from your company knowledge base. For small teams that want one source of truth, it's hard to beat.

Try Notion →
AI add-on from €9/mo

How to Choose

Pick ClickUp if — you need task management, workflows, and team coordination right away. The free tier is generous and you'll be up and running in an afternoon.

Pick Notion if — documentation and knowledge management is your primary need, with project management as a secondary feature. Better for teams that think in documents rather than tasks.

Use both if — you can afford €18/month total. They complement each other well: ClickUp for tasks and workflows, Notion for documentation and company knowledge.
